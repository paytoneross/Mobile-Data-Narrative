Setlist Data Narrative

       Here we can find a word cloud for all opening songs, as well as the raw excel spreadsheet showing the full setlist for each show, dates ranging from 5/4/24 to 9/29/24.

       The setlists are always fun to look at. Pearl Jam's shows have gotten a lot shorter in recent years, the first show I went to of theirs was night 2 of Seattle in 2018, and that show had 36 songs with 2 encores. Kinda sad they are a lot shorter now, but to be fair playing 36 songs in one night does sound pretty exhausting, especially when you are as old as they are.

       You can watch the attached Youtube video from CBS Sunday Morning to see Eddie talk a little bit about their setlists and how he makes them. It's not a ton of info, but you can learn a little bit about how he does it and what he considers when choosing the songs. They also talk a bit about pre show rituals and what Eddie brings on tour with him which is pretty fun.

       The most important song in the setlist, in my opinion, is the opener. It's the most exciting song in the show, and openers have the most variety out of the key songs in the setlist. This tour there were 16 different opening songs played vs only 6 different closing songs. Below is a little word cloud with all the openers, the bigger the word the more times it was played.

Opener Word Cloud

My Conclusions

       We can see that Release is the most common opener, with Long Road and Of The Girl not far behind. There is a good variety of songs here which makes it exciting to see which one they will choose if you see them live. Some weird ones are also in there like Garden, which hadn't been played as in opener since 1993, but made a crazy comeback this tour 31 years later.

They played a show on my birthday, I really wanted to go but ended up deciding not to, and of course that was the one time in this entire tour that they played my favorite song. Classic.
